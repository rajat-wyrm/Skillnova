DSA Practice Guide for SkillNova Interns

SkillNova provides a dedicated DSA (Data Structures and Algorithms) practice section to help interns strengthen their problem-solving skills.

1. Getting Started
- Navigate to the "Projects" section from the sidebar.
- Select the DSA Practice module.
- Problems are categorized by difficulty: Easy, Medium, Hard.
- Each problem includes a description, input/output format, and constraints.

2. Topic Coverage
The DSA practice module covers the following topics:
- Arrays and Strings: Two pointers, sliding window, prefix sums.
- Linked Lists: Reversal, cycle detection, merge operations.
- Stacks and Queues: Monotonic stacks, BFS applications.
- Trees and Graphs: DFS, BFS, shortest paths, topological sort.
- Dynamic Programming: Memoization, tabulation, common patterns.
- Sorting and Searching: Binary search, merge sort, quick sort.
- Hashing: HashMap patterns, frequency counting, anagram detection.

3. Practice Tips
- Start with Easy problems to build confidence.
- Focus on understanding the approach, not just the solution.
- Time yourself: aim for 20 minutes (Easy), 40 minutes (Medium), 60 minutes (Hard).
- Review editorial solutions after attempting each problem.
- Practice at least 2-3 problems daily for consistent improvement.

4. Assessment
- DSA performance is tracked as part of your Technical Skills evaluation.
- Weekly DSA challenges are posted every Monday.
- Top performers are recognized in the weekly standup meeting.
- DSA scores contribute to your overall internship performance rating.

5. Resources
- LeetCode and HackerRank links are provided for additional practice.
- Video explanations are available for complex topics.
- Peer discussion is encouraged through the Q&A Forum.
